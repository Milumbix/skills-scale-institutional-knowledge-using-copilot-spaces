# Stakeholder Engagement Plan

This template provides a structured approach to identifying, engaging, and communicating with project stakeholders throughout the project lifecycle.

---

## Purpose

The stakeholder engagement plan ensures:
- All relevant stakeholders are identified and mapped
- Communication is tailored to stakeholder needs and preferences
- Feedback loops are established and maintained
- Decision-making and approval processes are clear
- Expectations are managed proactively

---

Use this template to document all project stakeholders:

|------------------|----------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| [Name] | [Job title/team] | High/Med/Low | High/Med/Low | Weekly/Bi-weekly/Monthly/Milestone | Email/Slack/Meeting | [List concerns] | [How to engage] |

**Influence Level**:
- **High**: Decision-maker, budget owner, executive sponsor, can block/approve project
- **Medium**: Input provider, subject matter expert, affected by outcomes
- **Low**: Informed party, peripheral interest

**Interest Level**:
- **High**: Directly affected, invested in outcomes, actively engaged
- **Medium**: Indirectly affected, occasional engagement needed
- **Low**: Minimal impact, awareness only

### Stakeholder Quadrants

Plot stakeholders to determine engagement approach:

- **High Influence + High Interest**: **Manage Closely** - Regular 1:1s, involve in decisions, frequent updates
- **High Influence + Low Interest**: **Keep Satisfied** - Concise updates, escalate only critical issues
- **Low Influence + High Interest**: **Keep Informed** - Regular updates, open feedback channels
- **Low Influence + Low Interest**: **Monitor** - Periodic updates, minimal engagement

## Communication Plan

### Objectives
- Maintain alignment on project goals, timelines, and priorities
- Proactively communicate risks, issues, and changes
- Gather feedback and input to inform decisions
- Build trust and transparency across stakeholders
- Minimize surprises and manage expectations

### Communication Types & Cadence

#### 1. Status Updates
- **Frequency**: Weekly
- **Audience**: All high-interest stakeholders
- **Format**: Written summary (email/Slack)
- **Content**:
  - Progress this week
  - Upcoming milestones
  - Top 3 risks
  - Decisions needed
  - Wins/achievements

#### 2. Stakeholder Reviews
- **Frequency**: Bi-weekly or at major milestones
- **Audience**: High-influence stakeholders
- **Format**: 30-45 min meeting with deck
- **Content**:
  - Project health dashboard
  - Demo of recent progress
  - Roadmap and timeline updates
  - Risk review
  - Q&A and feedback

#### 3. Executive Briefings
- **Frequency**: Monthly or milestone-based
- **Audience**: Executive sponsors, senior leadership
- **Format**: Concise 1-pager or 15-min briefing
- **Content**:
  - Status (on-track/at-risk/off-track)
  - Key metrics and outcomes
  - Critical risks requiring executive attention
  - Budget status
  - Decisions needed

#### 4. Working Sessions
- **Frequency**: As needed
- **Audience**: Subject matter experts, affected teams
- **Format**: Collaborative workshop or deep dive
- **Content**:
  - Technical design reviews
  - Requirements gathering
  - Problem-solving sessions
  - User testing and feedback

#### 5. Launch Communications
- **Frequency**: One-time at launch
- **Audience**: All stakeholders + broader organization
- **Format**: Announcement email + documentation
- **Content**:
  - What's launching and why
  - How to access/use
  - Training resources
  - Support channels
  - Success metrics

## Feedback Loop

Establish clear channels for stakeholders to provide input:

### Feedback Collection Methods

- [ ] Regular stakeholder survey (quarterly)
- [ ] Open office hours or Q&A sessions
- [ ] Dedicated Slack channel for questions
- [ ] Feedback forms or suggestion boxes
- [ ] One-on-one stakeholder interviews
- [ ] Retrospective input from cross-functional teams

### Feedback Processing

1. **Capture**: Log all feedback in a central location (GitHub Issues, Notion, etc.)
2. **Acknowledge**: Respond within 48 hours to confirm receipt
3. **Analyze**: Categorize and prioritize feedback (feature request, bug, concern, question)
4. **Act**: Incorporate into backlog, roadmap, or immediate action items
5. **Close the loop**: Communicate back to stakeholder on outcome or decision

- Time to acknowledge feedback: < 48 hours
- Feedback incorporation rate: > 70% of high-priority feedback addressed

## Escalation & Approval Points

Define clear paths for escalations and approvals:

### Decision Authority Matrix

| Decision Type | Primary Decision Maker | Consulted | Informed | Approval Required |
|---------------|----------------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| Scope changes (minor) | Product Manager | Project Manager, Tech Lead | Stakeholders | No |
| Scope changes (major) | Product Director | PM, Engineering Lead | Executive Sponsor | Yes - Product Director |
| Budget increases | Project Manager | Finance | Executive Sponsor | Yes - Executive Sponsor |
| Timeline extensions | Project Manager | Product Manager, Engineering | Executive Sponsor | Yes - Product Director |
| Technical architecture | Tech Lead | Engineering Team | Project Manager | No (review only) |
| Go/No-Go for launch | Product Director | QA Lead, Engineering Lead | Executive Sponsor | Yes - Product Director |
| Risk response (critical) | Project Manager | Risk Owner | Product Lead, Exec Sponsor | Yes - Product Lead |

### Escalation Path

**Level 1 - Team Level**:
- Issues: Day-to-day blockers, technical questions
- Handled by: Project Manager, Tech Lead, Product Manager
- Response time: < 24 hours

**Level 2 - Product/Engineering Leadership**:
- Issues: Scope changes, timeline risks, resource constraints
- Handled by: Product Director, Engineering Director
- Response time: < 48 hours

**Level 3 - Executive Level**:
- Issues: Strategic pivots, major budget impacts, critical risks
- Handled by: Executive Sponsor, VP/C-level
- Response time: < 1 week

## Engagement Best Practices

- **Start early**: Engage stakeholders during project initiation, not just before launch
- **Be proactive**: Share updates before stakeholders have to ask
- **Tailor communication**: Executives want summaries; ICs want details
- **Use visuals**: Dashboards, roadmaps, and demos are more engaging than text
- **Listen actively**: Stakeholder concerns often reveal hidden risks
- **Respect time**: Keep meetings focused and end early if possible
- **Document decisions**: Clear audit trail prevents revisiting settled questions
- **Celebrate wins**: Share successes and recognize contributions

## Stakeholder Engagement Checklist

### Project Initiation (Week 1)
- [ ] Complete stakeholder identification and mapping
- [ ] Schedule kickoff meeting with key stakeholders
- [ ] Establish communication cadence and channels
- [ ] Create distribution lists or Slack channels
- [ ] Document decision authority matrix
- [ ] Get approval on engagement plan

### During Execution (Ongoing)
- [ ] Send weekly status updates
- [ ] Hold stakeholder reviews at planned intervals
- [ ] Track and respond to feedback within SLAs
- [ ] Update stakeholder matrix if roles change
- [ ] Escalate issues following defined paths
- [ ] Adjust communication plan based on feedback

### Milestones & Key Decisions
- [ ] Schedule milestone reviews 2 weeks in advance
- [ ] Prepare demo/materials 1 week before review
- [ ] Document decisions and share notes within 24 hours
- [ ] Get required approvals before proceeding
- [ ] Communicate outcomes to all stakeholders

### Launch Preparation (2 Weeks Before)
- [ ] Final stakeholder review and sign-off
- [ ] Prepare launch communications
- [ ] Train customer-facing teams (support, success, sales)
- [ ] Schedule post-launch retrospective with stakeholders
