# Change Adoption Checklist

This checklist provides a structured approach for Change Champions to drive successful adoption of new processes, tools, or features across the organization.

---

## Purpose

This checklist helps Change Champions:
- Assess organizational readiness for change initiatives
- Plan comprehensive communications and training programs
- Track adoption metrics and identify barriers
- Define clear go/no-go criteria for change rollouts
- Gather and act on user feedback to improve adoption

---

## Readiness Assessment

Complete this assessment 4-6 weeks before planned change rollout:

### 1. Change Impact Analysis
- [ ] Identify all user groups affected by the change
- [ ] Assess magnitude of change (minor tweak vs. major workflow shift)
- [ ] Determine number of users impacted (10 users vs. 1000 users)
- [ ] Evaluate current process maturity (replacing chaos vs. replacing working system)
- [ ] Identify key stakeholders who must support the change
- [ ] Document benefits and value proposition for each user group

### 2. Organizational Readiness
- [ ] Assess change fatigue (how many recent changes have users experienced?)
- [ ] Evaluate leadership support and commitment
- [ ] Determine resource availability (trainers, support staff, budget)
- [ ] Review cultural factors (openness to change, past change success rate)
- [ ] Identify change champions in each affected team
- [ ] Assess technical readiness (infrastructure, tools, integrations)

### 3. Risk & Resistance Assessment
- [ ] Identify potential sources of resistance (comfort with status quo, skill gaps, competing priorities)
- [ ] Assess risk of adoption failure and impact if change doesn't take hold
- [ ] Determine mitigation strategies for anticipated resistance
- [ ] Plan for rollback or phased approach if needed
- [ ] Identify early adopters who can model desired behavior

### 4. Readiness Score

Rate each dimension 1-5 (1=not ready, 5=highly ready):

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Leadership support | [1-5] | [Comments] |
| User need/pain point | [1-5] | [Comments] |
| Resource availability | [1-5] | [Comments] |
| Technical readiness | [1-5] | [Comments] |
| Change capacity | [1-5] | [Comments] |
| **Overall Readiness** | [Avg] | **>4.0 = Green, 3.0-4.0 = Yellow, <3.0 = Red** |

**Recommendation**: 
- **Green**: Proceed with rollout as planned
- **Yellow**: Address gaps before proceeding or plan phased rollout
- **Red**: Delay rollout until readiness improves

## Training Plan

Develop comprehensive training to ensure users can successfully adopt the change:

### Training Needs Analysis
- [ ] Identify knowledge gaps between current state and desired state
- [ ] Determine skill levels of different user groups
- [ ] Assess preferred learning styles (hands-on, video, documentation)
- [ ] Identify users who need basic vs. advanced training
- [ ] Determine if just-in-time training or advance training is better

### Training Materials Development

Create training assets:
- [ ] **Quick start guide** (1-page cheat sheet for basic use)
- [ ] **Detailed user documentation** (comprehensive how-to guide)
- [ ] **Video tutorials** (5-10 min demos for key workflows)
- [ ] **FAQs** (anticipated questions and answers)
- [ ] **Practice environment** (sandbox for hands-on learning)
- [ ] **Use case examples** (real-world scenarios relevant to users)

### Training Delivery Plan

| User Group | Training Format | Duration | Schedule | Trainer | Attendance Target |
|------------|----------------|----------|----------|---------|------------------|
| [Group] | [Live session / Self-paced / Office hours] | [Time] | [Date] | [Name] | [%] |

### Training Checklist
- [ ] Training materials completed 2 weeks before rollout
- [ ] Training sessions scheduled and communicated
- [ ] Trainers identified and prepared
- [ ] Practice environment set up and tested
- [ ] Feedback mechanism established for training quality
- [ ] Post-training assessment or quiz (optional)
- [ ] Recorded sessions available for on-demand access
- [ ] Training completion tracked

## Communications Plan

Create a comprehensive communications strategy to build awareness and excitement:

### Communication Objectives
- Build awareness of the upcoming change and its benefits
- Create urgency and motivation to adopt
- Provide clear instructions on how to prepare
- Address concerns and build confidence
- Celebrate early wins and momentum

### Communication Timeline

**6 Weeks Before Launch**:
- [ ] Initial announcement from leadership (what's changing and why)
- [ ] Teaser communications highlighting benefits
- [ ] Feedback channels opened for questions and concerns

**4 Weeks Before Launch**:
- [ ] Detailed information about the change shared
- [ ] Training schedule published
- [ ] Success stories or early adopter testimonials
- [ ] FAQ published and regularly updated

**2 Weeks Before Launch**:
- [ ] Final reminder about training opportunities
- [ ] Step-by-step "what to expect" guide
- [ ] Support resources and channels communicated
- [ ] Countdown communications to build momentum

**Launch Week**:
- [ ] Go-live announcement with clear instructions
- [ ] Launch event or demo (optional)
- [ ] Support team on high alert for questions
- [ ] Daily check-ins and tips shared

**Post-Launch (First 30 Days)**:
- [ ] Weekly adoption metrics shared
- [ ] Quick tips and best practices
- [ ] Success stories and wins celebrated
- [ ] Ongoing support and office hours promoted

### Communication Channels

Select channels based on audience preferences:
- [ ] Email newsletters or announcements
- [ ] Slack/Teams channels dedicated to the change
- [ ] Town hall or all-hands presentations
- [ ] Manager cascades (leaders brief their teams)
- [ ] Intranet or wiki page
- [ ] Video messages from executives
- [ ] Posters or visual reminders (if physical workspace)

### Communication Templates

**Announcement Template**:

### Leading Indicators (Early Warning Signs)

Monitor these to identify adoption issues early:
- [ ] Low training registration rate (< 50% by 1 week before launch)
- [ ] High volume of negative feedback or concerns
- [ ] Low engagement with pre-launch communications
- [ ] Key influencers or leaders not bought in
- [ ] Technical issues blocking access or use

### Lagging Indicators (Outcome Measures)

Track these to assess overall adoption success:
- [ ] % of users actively using the new tool/process
- [ ] % of users who have stopped using the old method
- [ ] Productivity metrics (time saved, errors reduced)
- [ ] Business outcomes achieved (costs reduced, quality improved)
- [ ] User satisfaction and NPS scores

## Go/No-Go Criteria

Use these criteria to decide whether to proceed with rollout:

### Green Light Criteria (Must Have)
- [ ] Leadership approval confirmed
- [ ] Training materials completed and tested
- [ ] Support resources staffed and ready
- [ ] Technical environment tested and stable
- [ ] Communication plan executed
- [ ] At least 70% of users aware of upcoming change
- [ ] Rollback plan documented and tested
- [ ] Critical blockers resolved

### Yellow Light Criteria (Proceed with Caution)
- [ ] Training completion rate < 80% but > 50%
- [ ] Some technical issues remain but have workarounds
- [ ] Moderate resistance identified but mitigation in place
- [ ] Support resources adequate but not ideal

### Red Light Criteria (Delay Launch)
- [ ] Critical technical issues unresolved
- [ ] Leadership support withdrawn or uncertain
- [ ] Training completion rate < 50%
- [ ] Significant user resistance with no mitigation
- [ ] Support resources not available
- [ ] Major competing priority emerges

## Feedback Collection Methods

Establish multiple channels to gather user feedback:

### During Rollout (First 30 Days)

**Quantitative Feedback**:
- [ ] Usage analytics (login frequency, feature usage, time spent)
- [ ] Pulse surveys (1-2 questions, weekly)
- [ ] Support ticket analysis (volume, themes, resolution time)
- [ ] Error rates and technical issues

**Qualitative Feedback**:
- [ ] Office hours or Q&A sessions
- [ ] One-on-one user interviews (sample of 10-15 users)
- [ ] Slack/Teams channel for questions and discussion
- [ ] Suggestion box or feedback form
- [ ] Observations and shadowing (for workflow changes)

### Post-Rollout (30-90 Days)

**Comprehensive Assessment**:
- [ ] Detailed user satisfaction survey (10-15 questions)
- [ ] Focus groups with different user segments
- [ ] Retrospective with project team and stakeholders
- [ ] Business impact analysis (did we achieve goals?)
- [ ] Lessons learned documentation

### Feedback Processing

1. **Collect**: Centralize all feedback in one location
2. **Categorize**: Bugs, feature requests, training gaps, usability issues
3. **Prioritize**: Impact vs. effort matrix
4. **Act**: Quick wins addressed immediately, larger items to backlog
5. **Close the loop**: Communicate back what was done or why not

## Adoption Support Plan

Provide ongoing support to ensure sustained adoption:

### Launch Support (First Week)
- [ ] Dedicated support team on standby
- [ ] Daily check-ins with each user group
- [ ] Real-time issue resolution (< 4 hour response time)
- [ ] Extra training sessions or office hours if needed
- [ ] Leadership visibility and encouragement

### Ongoing Support (Weeks 2-8)
- [ ] Weekly office hours for Q&A
- [ ] FAQ updates based on common questions
- [ ] Tips and tricks shared regularly
- [ ] Power user program to build internal advocates
- [ ] Continued monitoring of metrics and issues

### Sustaining Support (Months 3+)
- [ ] Transition to standard support channels
- [ ] Quarterly check-ins on adoption and satisfaction
- [ ] Continuous improvement based on feedback
- [ ] New user onboarding process for future hires
- [ ] Refresher training or advanced workshops

## Rollback Plan

Prepare for the possibility that adoption may not succeed:

### Rollback Triggers
- [ ] Adoption rate < 30% after 30 days
- [ ] Critical technical issues causing widespread problems
- [ ] Significant negative business impact (productivity loss, revenue impact)
- [ ] Major user revolt or leadership directive to stop

### Rollback Procedure
- [ ] Communication plan for announcing rollback
- [ ] Technical steps to revert to previous state
- [ ] Data migration or preservation plan
- [ ] Support for users transitioning back
- [ ] Post-mortem to understand what went wrong

## Change Champion Checklist

Use this quick checklist to ensure all bases are covered:

### 6 Weeks Before Launch
- [ ] Readiness assessment completed
- [ ] Training plan finalized
- [ ] Communication plan approved
- [ ] Adoption metrics defined
- [ ] Go/no-go criteria established

### 4 Weeks Before Launch
- [ ] Training materials completed
- [ ] Communications started
- [ ] Feedback channels opened
- [ ] Support team briefed

### 2 Weeks Before Launch
- [ ] Training sessions delivered
- [ ] Go/no-go decision made
- [ ] Final communications sent
- [ ] Support team on standby

### Launch Day
- [ ] Launch announcement sent
- [ ] Support monitoring intensified
- [ ] Metrics tracking started
- [ ] Quick wins celebrated

### First 30 Days
- [ ] Weekly adoption metrics reviewed
- [ ] User feedback collected and acted on
- [ ] Support provided as needed
- [ ] Adjustments made based on learnings

### 30-90 Days Post-Launch
- [ ] Comprehensive adoption assessment
- [ ] Sustained support plan in place
- [ ] Lessons learned documented
- [ ] Transition to business-as-usual
